Motivation

To easily classify the works of authors rather than doing exegesis manually

  • Naive Bayes Classifier
    • Naive Bayes Classifier involves first separating the document into training and test set. Then calculating the prior and conditional probabilities for the training set. And then calculating probabilities of test set with conditional probability value of training set.
    • Disadvantages with this approach
      1. Naive Bayes classifier makes a very strong assumption on the shape of your data distribution, i.e. any two features are independent given the output class. Due to this, the result can be (potentially) very bad - hence, a “naive” classifier.
      2. Another problem happens due to data scarcity. For any possible value of a feature, you need to estimate a likelihood value by a frequentist approach. This can result in probabilities going towards 0 or 1, which in turn leads to numerical instabilities and worse results. In this case, you need to smooth in some way your probabilities.
  • Logistic Regression
    • We have implemented a standard logistic regression model in this project. We aim at obtaining weights which could maximize the likeliness or likelihood of obtaining our data and model it in such a way that it is able to clearly distinguish the target values.
  • K Nearest Neigbors
      KNN technique involves calculating the TFIDF scores of all unique words for each author corpus. TFIDF scores of all unique words is computed for each author in test corpus. Euclidean distance is calculated by comparing the selected test document TFIDF scores with TFIDF scores of each document present in the training set.
